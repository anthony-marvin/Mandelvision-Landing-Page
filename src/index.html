What is LASIK?

LASIK laser eye surgery is the most commonly performed procedure for laser vision correction. It can help correct vision for the vast majority of refractive errors such as nearsightedness (myopia), farsightedness (hyperopia), and astigmatism.

During blade-free all-laser LASIK, a paper-thin flap of the cornea is created using the IntraLase® femtosecond laser. This gives a custom-designed laser access to the corneal tissue underneath. The VISX® Star S4 excimer laser is then used to re-profile the cornea to correct the refractive error of the eye. Dr. Mandel began researching the excimer laser at Columbia University in 1984, more than a decade before it received approval from the FDA for use in human treatment.

Once the laser treatment is completed, the flap is then gently repositioned over the re-profiled cornea. This replacement of the flap after the laser treatment allows for a fast recovery. In fact, the day after LASIK eye surgery, most patients are able to return to work, exercise and resume most of their normal daily activities.

LASIK can also help treat presbyopia, or the need for reading glasses after the age of 40. This type of LASIK is known as monovision LASIK.

Flex Spending

It's flexible spending time again!  The good news for those of you considering LASIK is that you can use money from your flexible spending account (FSA) to pay for a portion of your LASIK procedure.

Here’s a quick review of FSA’s for those of you who are not familiar with the benefits:
An FSA is an account set up by employers which allows employees to set aside tax-free dollars.  By putting this money aside, the employee pays no Federal, FICA or State taxes on the amount in their FSA account. This money can then be used to pay for many healthcare costs. Elective or cosmetic procedures, such as LASIK, are some of the items that can be paid for with this tax-free money.

Not sure if you have an FSA?
Check with your human resources department as they typically administer these plans.
